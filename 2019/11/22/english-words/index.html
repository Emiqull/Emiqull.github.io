rather

word class : adverb
phonetic symbol : UK /ˈrɑː.ðər/ US /ˈræð.ɚ/
explain : quite; to a slight degree
examples :

  • That’s rather a difficult book, here’s an easier one for you.
  • The train was rather too crowded for a comfortable journey.

(rather than)
explain : instead of; used especially when you perfer one thing to another.
example : I think i’d like to stay at home this evening rather than go out.

(rather you than me)
explain : said by someone who does not want to do the thing that someone else is doing.
example : “I’m having two teeth out next week.” “Rather you than me.”

polite

word class : adjective
phonetic symbol : UK /pəˈlaɪt/ US /pəˈlaɪt/
explain : behaving in a way that is socially correct and shows understanding of and care for other people’s feelings.
examples :

  • She sent me a polite letter thanking me for my inviation.
  • He was too polite to point out my mistake.

particular

word class : adjective
phonetic symbol : UK /pəˈtɪk.jə.lər/ US /pɚˈtɪk.jə.lɚ/
explain : special, or this and not other.
example : “Why did you ask?” “Oh, no particular reason, just making conversation.”

(in particular)
explain : especially
example : Are you looking for anything in particular?

phrase

word class : noun
phonetic symbol : UK /freɪz/ US /freɪz/
explain : a group of words that is part of, rather than the whole of, a sentence.
example : an adjectival phrase.

abbreviation

word class : noun
phonetic symbol : UK /əˌbriː.viˈeɪ.ʃən/ US /əˌbriː.viˈeɪ.ʃən/
explain : a short from of a word or phrase
examples :

  • Sorry - could you explain what some of these abbreviations mean in your letter?
  • These is a list of abbreviations near the beginning of the book.

etc.

phonetic symbol : UK /ɪtˈset.ər.ə/ US /ˈset̬.ɚ.ər.ə/
explain : abbreviation for et cetera: and other similar things. It is used to avoid giving a complete list.
examples :

  • we saw lot of lions, tigers, elephants, etc.
  • They ask for your personal details - age, sex, nationality, etc.

gate

word class : noun
phonetic symbol : UK /ɡeɪt/ US /ɡeɪt/
explain[1] : a part of fence or outside wall that is fixed at one side and opens and closes like a door, usually made of metal or wooden strips.

explain[2] : a similar device that slides across an opening, often folding into a smaller space as it is opened.
example : The lift won’t be move if the safety gate isn’t shut properly.

entrance

word class : noun
phonetic symbol : UK /ˈen.trəns/ US /ˈen.trəns/
explain : a door, gate, etc. by which you can enter a building or place.
example : There are two entrances, one at the front and one around the back.

sand

word class : noun
phonetic symbol : UK /sænd/ US /sænd/
explain : a substance that consists of very small grains of rock, found on beaches and in deserts.
example : The children played all day on/in the sand.

huge

word class : adjective
phonetic symbol : UK /hjuːdʒ/ US /hjuːdʒ/
explain : extremely large in size or amount.
examples :

  • His last three movies have all been huge successes.
  • They live in a huge house.

anger

word class : noun
phonetic symbol : UK /ˈæŋ.ɡər/ US /ˈæŋ.ɡɚ/
explain : a strong feeling that makes you want to hurt someone or be unpleasant because of something unfair or unkind that has happened.
examples :

  • He found it hard to contain (= control) his anger.
  • The people showed no surprise or anger at their treatment.

amount

word class : noun
phonetic symbol : UK /əˈmaʊnt/ US /əˈmaʊnt/
explain : a collection or mass, especially of something that can not be counted.
examples :

  • They did not deliver the right of amount of sand.
  • The new tax caused a huge amount of public anger.

loan

word class : noun
phonetic symbol : UK /ləʊn/ US /loʊn/
explain : an amount of money that is borrowed, often from a bank, and has to paid back, usually together with an extra amount of money that you have to pay as a charge for borrowing.
examples :

  • She is trying to get a $50,000 loan to start her own business.
  • We could apply for/take out a loan to buy a car.

priority

word class : noun
phonetic symbol : UK /praɪˈɒr.ə.ti/ US /praɪˈɔːr.ə.t̬i/
explain : something that is very important and must be dealt before other things.
examples :

  • My first/top priority is to find somewhere to live.
  • Banks normally give priority to large businesses when deciding on loans.

opposition

word class : noun
phonetic symbol : UK /ˌɒp.əˈzɪʃ.ən/ US /ˌɑː.pəˈzɪʃ.ən/
explain : strong disagreement.
example : There is a lot of opposition to the proposed changes.

(the opposition)
explain : the team or person being played against in a sports competition.
example : The opposition has/have some good players so it should be a tough match.

alternative

word class : adjective
phonetic symbol : UK /ɒlˈtɜː.nə.tɪv/ US /ɑːlˈtɝː.nə.t̬ɪv/
explain[1] : an alternative plan or method is one that you can use if you do not want to use another one.
example : the opposition parties have so far failed to set out an alternative strategy.

explain[2] : offering a choice between two or more things.
example : people stay because they do not have alternative opportunities.

opinion

word class : noun
phonetic symbol : UK /əˈpɪn.jən/ US /əˈpɪn.jən/
explain[1] : a thought or belief about something or someone.
examples :

  • He is very much of the opinion that alternative medicine is a waste of time.
  • What is your opinion about/on the matter?

explain[2] : a judgment about someone or something.
examples :

  • He has a very high opinion of himself(= think he is very skilled/clever in a way that is very annoying).
  • She has a good/high opinion of his abilities(= think he is good).

traditional

word class : adjective
phonetic symbol : UK /trəˈdɪʃ.ən.əl/ US /trəˈdɪʃ.ən.əl/
explain : following or belonging to the customs or ways of behaving that have continued in a group of people or society for along time without changing.
examples :

  • She is very traditional in her ideas and opinions.
  • The dancers were wearing traditional Hungarian dress/costume.

election

word class : noun
phonetic symbol : UK /iˈlek.ʃən/ US /iˈlek.ʃən/
explain : a time when people vote in order to chose someone for a political or official job.
examples :

  • The government is expected to call an election(= allow the country to vote) very soon.
  • Local government elections will take place in May.

rare

word class : adjective
phonetic symbol : UK /reər/ US /rer/
explain[1] : not common for frequent; very unusual.
example : It is very rare to find these birds in New England in winter.

explain[2] : (of meat) not cooked for very long and still red.
example : I’d like my steak rare, please.

explain[3] : used to describe the air at the top of the a mountain, which contains less oxygen, making it harder to breathe.

influence

word class[1] : noun
phonetic symbol : UK /ˈɪn.flu.əns/ US /ˈɪn.flu.əns/
explain : the power to have an effect on people or things, or a person or thing that is able to do this:
examples :

  • Helen’s a bad/good influence on him.
  • At the time she was under the influence of her father.

word class[2]: verb
explain : to affect or change how someone or something develops, behaves, or thinks.
examples :

  • She’s very good at making friends and influencing people.
  • What influenced you to choose a career in nursing?

vulnerability

word class : noun
phonetic symbol : UK /ˌvʌl.nər.əˈbɪl.ə.ti/ /ˌvʌn.rəˈbɪl.ə.ti/ US /ˌvʌl.nɚ.əˈbɪl.ə.t̬i/
explain : the quality of being vulnerable (= able to be easily hurt, influenced, or attacked), or something that is vulnerable.
examples :

  • You want a doctor who understands the patient’s vulnerability.
  • The vote exposed vulnerabilities in the election process.
